San Antonio Independent School District v. Rodriguez

  • Happened 1973.
  • In San Antonio tax money received by schools was based on taxes in the local area, meaning richer districts received more money and poorer districts received less.
  • Demetrio Rodriguez and other parents of the Edgewood Independent School District argued locally based education taxes violated the 14th amendment's equal protection clause.
  • Parents filed a lawsuit against 7 other school districts, the Bexar County School Trustees, and the State of Texas itself.
  • Eventually the Bexar County School Trustees and the school districts were dropped from the case, leaving only the State of Texas as the defendant.
  • The Supreme Court decided by a narrow majority that the right to education was neither "explicitly nor implicitly" found anywhere in the Constitution, and therefore was not protected by it, meaning Rodriguez lost the case.
